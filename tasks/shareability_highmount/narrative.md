# Shareability - Narrative Brief (Highmount Target)

## One-Liner

The company YouTube hires to do YouTube, now structured as an intelligence platform + venture engine that can materially accelerate Highmount's ecosystem.

---

## The Problem (Beat 1: Why Now, Why Urgent)

YouTube is now the cultural epicenter for sports and entertainment. The winners in this market are the groups who understand YouTube's mechanics, creator ecosystems, and performance architecture at a granular level.

Most organizations operate blind on the platform. They lack:
- Intelligence on what's actually working (not vanity metrics)
- Strategy infrastructure that translates platform dynamics into growth
- Repeatable systems for packaging talent into investable opportunities

**Timing trigger:** YouTube dominance is accelerating. The gap between "gets YouTube" and "doesn't get YouTube" is widening. Organizations that solve this now will own the next era of sports and entertainment.

**Who feels it:** Leagues, IP owners, talent management companies, and ecosystem builders like Highmount who need to scale creator networks and audience growth systematically.

---

## The Insight (Beat 2: What They See That Others Don't)

Shareability sits at the intersection of two valuable positions:

1. **Platform proximity:** They are the company YouTube hires to do YouTube. This isn't consultants advising from outside; they help shape how major initiatives get built, measured, and activated from inside the platform relationship.

2. **Intelligence-to-execution separation:** The real value is in the intelligence and architecture, not the execution. Best-in-class operators should do execution. This insight creates a partnership model where Shareability keeps the brain while partners get the hands.

**The non-obvious belief:** YouTube strategy is intelligence infrastructure, not a service. It should be owned, not rented. And it should feed into a flywheel that creates compounding value for operators, not just one-off campaigns.

---

## The Solution (Beat 3: Two Linked Assets)

Shareability is structured as two tightly linked assets:

### Asset 1: Shareability Platform

YouTube-first intelligence, strategy, and performance infrastructure.

**What it does:**
- Trend intelligence that YouTube itself relies on
- Channel strategy and creator network expansion
- Distribution playbooks and performance architecture
- Audience growth systems

**Track record:** Olympics, NFL, Formula 1, PGA, Taylor Swift, Roblox, the Grammys, and more. These aren't client logos; this is YouTube bringing Shareability in to execute their most important cultural initiatives.

**Strategic direction:** Getting out of the execution game, keeping the intelligence and architecture. Looking to partner with best-in-class operators who can feed their services and operator flywheel for execution.

### Asset 2: Packaging and Venture Engine

A repeatable system that turns top-tier talent access into investable opportunities across content, experiences, and brands.

**What it does:**
- Takes talent relationships and turns them into structured deal flow
- Packages concepts through to commercialization
- Creates pipeline of investable celebrity IP opportunities

**Proof point:** Tim McGraw and Down Home. That success brought more top-level talent seeking their own opportunities.

**Paramount relationship:** Paramount and Paramount Sports are part of the pipeline. Not something to buy, but something that can become preferred access.

## The Ask (Beat 6: Two Clean Paths)

### Path A: Strategic Transaction

Acquire the Shareability Platform or take a controlling stake.

**What you get:**
- Full ownership/control of YouTube intelligence infrastructure
- Platform capabilities integrated into Highmount ecosystem

**What stays connected:**
- Preferred partnership structure keeps Shareability team connected to capabilities as needed

**Best for:** Highmount wanting to own the intelligence infrastructure outright.

### Path B: Venture Engine Investment

Invest directly into the packaging and venture group.

**What you get:**
- Capital participation in the pipeline and venture creation engine
- Execution opportunities flowing into Highmount orbit
- Preferred access to the Shareability Platform

**What it unlocks:**
- Your capital and operating ecosystem helps scale the deal flow system
- Structured deal-flow model where Highmount can run select opportunities through for improved economics and speed
- Carry-style economics participation

**Best for:** Highmount wanting partnership leverage without full acquisition.

### The Highmount Fit

Highmount and the Dude Perfect ecosystem are positioned as a highly credible execution partner for the kinds of initiatives Shareability's platform unlocks. This isn't just an investment opportunity; it's a flywheel accelerator.

## Unique Angles

- **YouTube-as-infrastructure:** This isn't about running a YouTube channel. It's about owning the intelligence layer that YouTube itself relies on.

- **The execution pivot:** Most pitch decks say "we're the best at execution." This one says "we're getting out of execution." That's rare honesty that signals maturity.

- **Operator flywheel:** The value prop isn't "hire us." It's "own the system that feeds your operators." That's equity-creating, not service-selling.

- **Paramount access without Paramount risk:** Getting deal flow benefits without M&A complexity.

- **Tim McGraw as proof, not pitch:** Down Home already happened. This isn't speculative.

---

## Contrarian Elements

1. **Against:** "We need to scale our execution capabilities."
   **Position:** No. Scale intelligence. Partner on execution.

2. **Against:** "YouTube is just another distribution channel."
   **Position:** YouTube is the cultural epicenter. Treat it as infrastructure, not a tactic.

3. **Against:** "We should buy Paramount access."
   **Position:** Access is a pipeline relationship, not an acquisition target.
